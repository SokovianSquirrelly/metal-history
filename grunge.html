Seattle's Grunge Scene

Also known as the "Seattle sound", grunge music and culture emerged during the mid-1980s in Washington state, specifically in and around Seattle. Grunge has a lot of influences from alternative rock, punk rock, early heavy metal, hardcore punk, and sludge metal. However, the fast speed of punk is practically non-existent. Instrumentals are heavily distorted and has what many would consider a "grungy" sound to it. Lyrical themes are typically angsty and addresses a lot of heavier introspective themes like betrayal, self-doubt, abuse, neglect, and trauma. Many of these lyrical themes became easy for teenagers to relate to and the genre became incredibly popular in the '90s.

Many notable grunge bands popped up in Seattle, such as Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains. Outside of Seattle, bands like Stone Temple pilots and The Smashing Pumpkins were also frontrunners in the genre. Some of these bands would heavily influence alternative metal, and eventually other subgenres like post-grunge. However, even though grunge was incredibly popular during the early '90s, the popularity of the genre was very short-lived. By the end of the decade, many grunge bands had either broken up or became more obscure. One of the largest hits to the culture and genre was the suicide of Nirvana frontman, Kurt Cobain, who had also struggled with substance abuse leading up to his death.

Even though the genre was so short-lived, it had an important impact on society outside of music. First off, it helped the city of Seattle. Before the 1990s, Seattle was a very different place than it is now. It was essentially a blue-collar logging town with no real music scene. Bands would skip Seattle when they were on tour because to them, it was just a bigger town that was just too far out of the way to justify scheduling a concert. In fact, the clothing style of grunge was reflective of the kind of city that Seattle was at the time, with flannel and doc martins. Fast forward to today, where Seattle has two big things going for it: being a tourist location and being a technology hub. Because grunge music made Seattle look like a hot and happening place, some of the biggest tech companies in the world emerged, such as Boeing, Microsoft, and Amazon. This change may not have happened overnight, but the city of Seattle has changed drastically since the 1990s thanks to grunge.

However, probably the most important change that grunge brought to society was the importance of introspection and self-exploration. Grunge resonated deeply with teenagers at the time, especially in Seattle. Not only were the lyrics introspective, but they were seen as down-to-earth. The musicians also went against the trend of glam metal at the time, where rather than dressing up in colorful spandex, the musicians looked like they just walked on stage with whatever they were wearing for the day. The musicians were more approachable, which seems weird to bring up, but before 1992, the concept of a "down-to-earth celebrity" was incredibly foreign. Teenagers saw themselves in these bands, especially with the likes of Nirvana's Kurt Cobain or Soundgarden's Chris Cornell. This was because those musicians were those teenagers that felt stranded in their "crappy home town" and struggled to find their place in life. Over the years, this introspective music carried over into other popular genres like metalcore, emo, and punk. As time went on, more and more important issues like mental health came to light, and it can be argued that society and pop culture has become more compassionate and accepting as a result.

Music Example: Nirvana - Smells Like Teen Spirit

After grunge had largely faded, a derivative of grunge called post-grunge would emerge. The sound was less abrasive than its parent genre, though still had a lot of thick, distorted guitars and introspective lyrics. Post-grunge is also largely intertwined with alternative metal, arguably more so than grunge ever was. Because of it being less abraisive, post-grunge is much more radio-friendly and is often nicknamed "butt rock". Notable post-grunge bands include Foo Fighters, Bush, Creed, Shinedown, Seether, and Nickelback.
