Seattle's Grunge Scene

Also known as the "Seattle sound", grunge music and culture emerged during the mid-1980s in Washington state, specifically in and around Seattle. Grunge has a lot of influences from alternative rock, punk rock, early heavy metal, hardcore punk, and sludge metal. However, the fast speed of punk is practically non-existent. Instrumentals are heavily distorted and has what many would consider a "grungy" sound to it. Lyrical themes are typically angsty and addresses a lot of heavier introspective themes like betrayal, self-doubt, abuse, neglect, and trauma. Many of these lyrical themes became easy for teenagers to relate to and the genre became incredibly popular in the '90s.

Many notable grunge bands popped up in Seattle, such as Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains. Outside of Seattle, bands like Stone Temple pilots and The Smashing Pumpkins were frontrunners in the genre. Some of these bands would heavily influence alternative metal, and eventually other subgenres like post-grunge. However, even though grunge was incredibly popular during the '90s, by the end of the century, many grunge bands had either broken up or became more obscure. One of the largest hits to the culture and genre was the suicide of Nirvana frontman, Kurt Cobain, who had also struggled with substance abuse leading up to his death. While the genre was short-lived, it brought a lot of important issues into pop culture and helped promote intropsection and self-exploration.

Music Example: Nirvana - Smells Like Teen Spirit

After grunge had largely faded, a derivative of grunge called post-grunge would emerge. The sound was less abrasive than its parent genre, though still had a lot of thick, distorted guitars and introspective lyrics. Post-grunge is also largely intertwined with alternative metal, arguably more so than grunge ever was. Notable post-grunge bands include Foo Fighters, Bush, Creed, Shinedown, Seether, and Nickelback.

Music Example: Creed - With Arms Wide Open
