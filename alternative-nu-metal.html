Alternative & Nu Metal

The mid-80s saw a new genre emerge as a mix between heavy metal and alternative rock, and sometimes even other genres that aren't generally associated with metal. This became known as alternative metal. Alternative metal bands often play with downtuned guitars, much like much of metal up to this point, but in contrast to genres like thrash metal, alternative metal guitar riffs are generally mid-paced. A mix of melodic vocals and harsh vocals is also common.

The genre first emerged in the mid-80s with bands like Faith No More, Jane's Addiction, and Soundgarden, but the genre wasn't popularized until the '90s when bands like Tool and Alice in Chains entered the scene. Today, other popular alternative metal bands include Breaking Benjamin, Evanescense, and Three Days Grace.

Music Example: Tool - Sober

The alternative metal scene in California spawned a new subgenre called nu metal. Nu metal is seen as a fusion between alternative metal and other non-metal subgenres, most commonly hip hop, funk, and grunge. While many other subgenres of metal show a large display of technical competence like guitar solos, nu metal fights against that trend hard. The music often has a syncopated rhythm often found in hip hop and funk. Vocal technique varies from artist to artist and these techniques include singing, screaming, growling, and even rapping. Some bands also feature a DJ that handles sampling and turntables.

Nu metal is one of the more divisive subgenres of heavy metal, being criticized for the "dumbing-down" of heavy metal or as being a desperate attempt to get into the mainstream. Some even question the legitimacy of nu metal even being a true subgenre of heavy metal due to bands being more "whiny" in contrast to bands in other metal subgenres that express music in a more visceral way. Despite all this, nu metal enjoyed huge success during the '90s and early-2000s and it put bands like Linkin Park, Limp Bizkit, Slipknot, Korn, System of a Down, Godsmack, Disturbed, and Kid Rock on the map. While nu metal had lost most of its popularity by around 2004, this also opened up the doors for others in metal. Metalcore was picking up momentum at this time and starting in the 2010s, nu metal went through a revival due to metalcore bands like Issues and Of Mice & Men mixing the two genres together to create nu metalcore. Aside from that, Slipknot, who was one of the heavier and more aggresive bands of this era, became a gateway for many elements of modern metalcore, deathcore, and death metal.
