New Wave of British Heavy Metal

Starting in the mid-70s, the United Kingdom had seen a boom of heavy metal bands inspired by the likes of Black Sabbath and Judas Priest. However, many of these bands didn't achieve the mainstream until the '80s where they recieved more media attention. Many of these bands were mixing in elements of Black Sabbath's heavy sound with the often fast and intense music of punk rock, and it sent a message that metal was here to stay and wasn't just a music fad from some guys in England. This also spawned branching subgenres in America and Europe like glam metal and thrash metal. Notable bands from this era included Iron Maiden and Def Leppard.

This was also a breakthrough for Ozzy Osbourne who quickly became a household name. Osbourne was already famous for being the frontman of the legendary Black Sabbath, but upon being dismissed from the band in the late-70s became a solo artist. His debut solo album "Blizzard of Ozz", held heavy metal anthems such as "Crazy Train" and "Mr. Crowley" and as of 2019 became 5x platinum (meaning it sold over 5 million copies). Today, Ozzy Osbourne holds the nickname "Prince of Darkness" due to his influence on heavy metal.
