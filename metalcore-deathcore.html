Metalcore & Deathcore

Metalcore is defined as a fusion genre between extreme metal and hardcore punk. It is noted for passages in music in which there are slow, heavy, intense passages of instrumentals conductive for moshing. These passages were often referred to as breakdowns. While other subgenres that combine metal and punk also have this element, metalcore is by far the most popular. Guitars would often have heavy riffs and sometimes stop-start rhythms, drummers would sometimes use blast beats, and vocalists would typically use thrash or screaming vocals.

When metalcore first started in America in the mid-90s, bands used more elements of hardcore punk than with heavy metal, especially in vocals. This music was rarely, if ever, melodic. This early version of metalcore was often known as "metallic hardcore", since it was essentially hardcore punk with metal guitar riffs. Notable bands from this era include Hatebreed, Poison the Well, Eighteen Visions, and Integrity.

Music Example: Eighteen Visions - Tower of Snakes

Starting in the early-2000s, metalcore started to shift in its style and influence, and also became more popular and approachable. Many bands of this era started to use more melodic vocals and instrumentation and their influence came from melodic death metal. This became known as "melodic metalcore", though later was mostly just known simply as "metalcore" since many bands stuck to the more melodic style of the 2000s. Notable bands from this era include Killswitch Engage, As I Lay Dying, Trivium, Bullet for My Valentine, and All That Remains. This also became a popular genre for "scene" bands such as Bring Me the Horizon, Asking Alexandria, and A Day to Remember.

Music Example: Killswitch Engage - My Curse

As the 2000s moved on, other subgenres of metalcore emerged, such as electronicore and mathcore, but the one with the biggest impact was deathcore. Deathcore is a fusion between metalcore and death metal, employing many of the same elements as metalcore though with more death growls and brutal instrumentation. Notable bands include Whitechapel, Suicide Silence, Chelsea Grin, Job For a Cowboy, and Carnifex. Deathcore was also popular for people who identified as "scene", especially with bands like Suicide Silence.

Unfortunately, this original scene of deathcore was pretty short-lived due to the untimely death of Suicide Silence vocalist, Mitch Lucker. Just like how Kurt Cobain was the icon of the grunge scene, Lucker was the icon of the deathcore scene in the early 2010s, so the death of an icon like Lucker was a hit to the scene. While Suicide Silence, along with many other deathcore bands at the time, did continue touring and writing music, they were unfortunately no longer at their peak.

However, in 2020 and 2021, deathcore started a revival due to two bands: Lorna Shore and Slaughter to Prevail. Both bands had been active for a number of years at this point, but they were nowhere near mainstream until the 2020s. For Lorna Shore, they released their song "To the Hellfire", which was the first song released with their new vocalist, Will Ramos. This song incorporated elements of black metal and symphonic metal into deathcore, and with the addition of Ramos's over-the-top vocals, it quickly grabbed the attention of the internet on platforms like TikTok and YouTube. Around the same time, Slaughter to Prevail saw similar success with their songs "Demolisher" and "Baba Yaga". Both songs also gained a lot of attention on YouTube and TikTok, which helped kickstart many other deathcore bands and their careers. Some of these other bands include Brand of Sacrifice, Ov Sulfur, Infant Annihilator, and Shadow of Intent.

Music Example: Whitechapel - The Saw is the Law
