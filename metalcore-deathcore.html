Metalcore & Deathcore

Metalcore is defined as a fusion genre between extreme metal and hardcore punk. It is noted for passages in music in which there are slow, heavy, intense passages of instrumentals conductive for moshing. While other subgenres that combine metal and punk also have this element, metalcore is by far the most popular. Guitars would often have heavy riffs and sometimes stop-start rhythms, drummers would sometimes use blast beats, and vocalists would typically use thrash or screaming vocals.

When metalcore first started in America in the mid-90s, bands used more elements of hardcore punk than with heavy metal, especially in vocals. This music was rarely, if ever, melodic. Notable bands from this era include Hatebreed, Poison the Well, Eighteen Visions, and Integrity.

Music Example: Eighteen Visions - Tower of Snakes

Starting in the early-2000s, metalcore started to shift in its style and influence, and also became more popular and approachable. Many bands of this era started to use more melodic vocals and instrumentation and their influence came from melodic death metal. This became known as "melodic metalcore", though later was mostly just known simply as "metalcore" since many bands stuck to the more melodic style of the 2000s. Notable bands from this era include Killswitch Engage, As I Lay Dying, Trivium, Bullet for My Valentine, and All That Remains. This also became a popular genre for "scene" bands such as Bring Me the Horizon, Asking Alexandria, and A Day to Remember.

Music Example: Killswitch Engage - My Curse

Metalcore also spawned sub-subgenres like deathcore, electronicore, easycore, and mathcore. Of these, deathcore is arguably the most popular. Deathcore is a fusion between metalcore and death metal, employing many of the same elements as metalcore though with more death growls and brutal instrumentation. Notable bands include Whitechapel, Suicide Silence, Chelsea Grin, Carnifex, Slaughter to Prevail, and later on, Lorna Shore. Deathcore was also popular for people who identified as "scene", especially with bands like Suicide Silence.

Music Example: Whitechapel - The Saw is the Law
