Miscellaneous Genres

These subgenres of metal, while they may be popular in certain parts of the world, don't have quite the same cultural impact as many other metal subgenres. However, they are worth mentioning as they are, in my opinion, still worth learning about.

Doom Metal

Candlemass performing in Wacken, Germany

While Black Sabbath were the godfathers of heavy metal, they also inspired another genre: doom metal. Doom metal is generally heavier, slower, and darker than most conventional metal and is meant to bring a sense of dread, despair, and impending doom. The genre started to pick up traction thanks to bands like Witchfinder General, Candlemass, Trouble, and Pentagram.

Even though doom metal is considered extreme metal, unlike other extreme metal subgenres like death metal or black metal, traditional doom metal vocalists use clean vocals, albeit with a depressing tone to convey the feeling of dread. However, sub-subgenres of doom metal that are often combined with other extreme subgenres may use harsh vocals like death growls or screaming.

Music Example: YOB - Unmask the Spectre

Folk Metal

Cemican

Folk metal is a fusion genre of heavy metal and traditional folk music. It was developed in Europe throughout the '90s like Skyclad from England and Cruachan from Ireland. More and more similar bands popped up in other countries, most notably in Finland in the early 2000s. Eventually, the genre also picked up a strong presence through the Middle East and into Northern Africa with bands such as Jerusalem's Melechesh, Lebanon's Kordz, India's Bloodywood, and Egypt's Massive Scar Era.

Though most popular in Europe, the Middle East, and Northern Africa, the genre has found itself gaining traction throughout the world as bands have incorporated their local cultures into their own music. For example, Mexican band Cemican uses pre-hispanic instruments fasioned after the ones used by the Aztec and Mayan Civilizations. Cemican's vocals, rather than being in the band's native Spanish are actually often in Mayan or Nahuatl and explore myths and legends of the ancient civilizations that lived in Mexico for centuries. When on stage, the band is often seen wearing body paint and feather-and-bone armor and headresses much like the ancient Aztec warriors.

The genre is characterized by the use of traditional instruments in conjunction with the standard instrumentation of heavy metal. Ocassionally, traditional singing styles are used as well. Lyrical themes include fantasy, mythology, and nature. The "metal" part of folk metal is quite diverse, as many different subgenres of metal are incorporated into this genre. For example, Cemican combines Aztec or Mayan music with melodic death metal. Bloodywood combines Indian folk music with nu metal. Myrath combines Middle Eastern and Arabic melodies with power metal or progressive metal. Many European folk metal bands like Moonsorrow use black metal with folk music.

Music Example: Moonsorrow - Unohduksen lapsi

Gala from The Hu performing in Nashville

While there are many notable and influential bands in folk metal, including those that were previously mentioned, as of 2022, one of the most relevant is the Mongolian folk metal band known as The Hu, which is actually an oddball to the world of folk metal. While most folk metal bands have a strong presence of heavy metal instrumentation and vocals in their music, The Hu primarily sticks with traditional Mongolian instruments and throat singing without losing the heaviness and intensity that comes with the parent genre. Much of folk metal is largely ignored in the United States, but The Hu is an exception to that rule, making its presence known and is even starting to have music featured in Hollywood and in high-profile video games such as EA's "Jedi: Fallen Order".

Music Example: The Hu - Yuve Yuve Yu

Groove Metal

Dimebag Darrel of Pantera performing in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada

In 1990, an album was released from Texas-based heavy metal band Pantera. This album, named "Cowboys from Hell", is often cited as the first groove metal album. This new style of metal evolved from thrash metal of the '80s and incorporated raspy or screaming vocals, down-tuned guitars, heavy guitar riffs, and syncopated rhythms. Even though groove metal evolved from thrash metal, it's often slower than its parent genre. Throughout the '90s groove metal spread to bands like White Zombie and Machine Head. Today, other notable bands of groove metal include Lamb of God, DevilDriver, and Five Finger Death Punch.

Music Example: Pantera - Walk

Industrial Metal

Rammstein performing in Wacken, Germany

Industrial metal is the fusion of heavy metal and industrial music. This music typically includes repeating metal riffs, synthesizers, sampling, and distorted vocals. The genre developed in the late '80s with bands like Nine Inch Nails, Rammstein, Ministry, Marilyn Manson, and Fear Factory. The genre picked up a lot of popularity in North America, but that popularity was short-lived, only lasting about a decade. By the late '90s to early 2000s, the genre received backlash from critics who felt that the industrail sound in heavy metal became too generic. Industrial metal bands tried to counter this by adding hip hop and electronic into their music, which helped influence nu metal. By this point, industrial metal had faded into obscurity.

Music Example: Rammstein - Du Hast

Power Metal

Joakim from Sabaton performing in Amsterdam

Power metal has its origins in the early '80s in North America with Rainbow's lyrical subject matter that revolved around medieval, renaissance, folk, and science fiction themes. American power metal was generally heavier than the later European power metal that would become much more popular. Most of power metal's influence has largely stayed in Europe, but that influence is spread across the continent.

Like glam metal, power metal fights against other subgenres of heavy metal with lighter and more uplifting music, sometimes with an anthem-like feel. However, unlike glam metal, power metal doesn't employ elements of pop music, rather using elements of speed metal instead. Power metal also has a different lyrical subject matter from glam metal's "drugs, sex, & rock n' roll" culture. Instead, power metal's lyrical themes are often fantasy-based with strong choruses and a dramatic, theatrical, and emotionally powerful sound. Power metal also often has a symphonic context to their sound. Notable power metal bands include Dragonforce, Sabaton, Helloween, and Blind Guardian.

Music Example: Dragonforce - Through the Fire and Flames

Symphonic Metal

Epica performing in Minneapolis

Symphonic metal has its origins across much of Europe, specifically in Sweden, Finland, and the Netherlands. It refers to heavy metal (or one of its subgenres) that has added orchestral and/or operatic elements. Some bands do feature orchestral instruments or choirs, while others may use keyboard orchestration. Many vocalists for symphonic metal bands were trained in classical and opera music so oftentimes they add the operatic element to the genre. Notable symphonic metal bands include Nightwish, Epica, Within Temptation, and Therion.

Music Example: Nightwish - Dark Chest of Wonders
