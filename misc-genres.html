Symphonic Metal

Symphonic metal has its origins across much of Europe, specifically in Sweden, Finland, and the Netherlands. It refers to heavy metal (or one of its subgenres) that has added orchestral and/or operatic elements. Some bands do feature orchestral instruments or choirs, while others may use keyboard orchestration. Many vocalists for symphonic metal bands have classically trained vocalists that add the operatic element to the genre. Notable symphonic metal bands include Nightwish, Epica, Within Temptation, and Therion.

Music Example: Nightwish - Dark Chest of Wonders
