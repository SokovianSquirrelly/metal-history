Hair Metal: Where Metal Met Pop

While many metal bands in the '80s were trying to go heavier and darker, there also came a subgenre that went the opposite direction. Rather than dark and heavy music of thrash metal, death metal, or black metal, hair metal, or more officially known as glam metal, fought against that trend. The music employed pop-inspired hooks and guitar riffs, upbeat rock anthems, and slower power ballads. Many glam metal musicians borrowed clothing style from the glam rock bands of the '70s, which used flashy clothing and makeup, spandex, lace, and often bright colors. This was in stark contrast to the thrash metal scene, which used denim, leather, and a "battle vest".

The genre has its origins in the United States, more specifically in Los Angeles. It became part of the Sunset Strip music scene especially with bands such as Mötley Crüe and Quiet Riot. At the same time, hair metal bands on the East Coast also spawned such as Bon Jovi and Twisted Sister. Other bands include Warrant, Ratt, Hanoi Rocks, and Cinderella.
