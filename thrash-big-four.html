Thrash & The Big Four

Thrash metal is defined by its more extreme take on heavy metal, being often faster and more aggresive than most conventional heavy metal at the time. Common elements of the genre include fast, percussive beats, a shredding lead guitar, and low-register guitar riffs. Lyrical themes were often critical about politics, environmental ruin, and occasionally Christianity. In a lot of ways, thrash metal lyrical themes were similar to those of hardcore punk. In fact, it's not wrong to say that thrash is a light fusion between hardcore punk and British heavy metal at the time.

This era also spawned the "Big Four", which is a term often given to Metallica, Megadeth, Slayer, and Anthrax. These bands were the most influential and commercially successful in the genre. In fact, as of 2022, Metallica is the most commercially successful band across all of heavy metal of all time. These bands helped to define and build the genre of thrash metal.

While the genre mostly popularized in the United States, especially in the San Francisco Bay Area, there also became a large scene of thrash metal in Germany with bands like Kreator, Destruction, Sodom, and Tankard. Other notable thrash metal bands include Sepultura, Testament, Overkill, and Exodus.

By the mid-90s, the genre had declined in popularity in favor of alternative metal, grunge, and nu metal. However, it did help to start a new genre during that time known as groove metal which was headed by Pantera.
