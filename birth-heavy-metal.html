The Birth of Heavy Metal

In 1970, a band from Birmingham, England called Black Sabbath released their debut album. Their music was darker and heavier than the raw sound of the '60s hard rock bands and it started a new genre that we know today as heavy metal. Upon this album being released, Deep Purple and Led Zeppelin also helped to pioneer the genre. They also made music that carried the heavier and thicker sound that started with Black Sabbath. This new musical phenomenon attracted audiences quickly despite critics deriding the new heavier music.

Heavy metal evolved quickly. Styles and theatrics like the shock rock of Alice Cooper and Kiss, the blues-rooted rock of Aerosmith, and the flashy guitar work of Van Halen were added to the genre, continuing to mold it into the genre it was to become.
