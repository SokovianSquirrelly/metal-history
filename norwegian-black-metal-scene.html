The Norwegian Black Metal Scene

Black metal is easily the most controversial subgenre of heavy metal due to the culture surrounding it. It's a form of extreme metal that commonly includes fast tempos, tremolo picking on guitars, dark lyrics, and shrieking vocals. In the early days of black metal, many bands used raw, lofi recordings, though much of modern black metal has abandoned that trait in their music. It's common to see black metal musicians in black and white face paint referred to as "corpse paint" and black clothing with spikes. Many black metal bands strive to hide from mainstream culture and remain underground.

Black metal didn't become immediately controversial. The subgenre came from death metal and thrash metal bands experimenting with their music and pushing the envelope on how intensely dark their music can be. Bands that were part of this "first wave" of black metal included Venom, Bathory, Hellhammer, and Mercyful Fate. The genre was even named after Venom's album, "Black Metal".

The genre didn't explode until the early 1990's in Norway and this "second wave" of black metal became known as the "Norwegian Black Metal Scene". This drew a lot of controversy throughout Norway and even throughout Europe due to the ideology of many black metal musicians, which included misanthropy, a practice of Satanism and/or ethnic paganism, and a strong hatred for Christianity. The scene attracted media attention due to these ideologies and also due to murders and church burnings in Norway. Some of the notable bands at this time included Mayhem, Darkthrone, Burzum, Emporer, and Gorgoroth.

Today, while many early black metal bands are still present, the subgenre has widely abandoned controversial practices, though their music still evokes dark and often Satanic themes. Unfortunately, a niche group of black metal musicians who are considered National Socialst Black Metal, or NSBM, carry extreme views on Fascism, anti-semitism, and neo-Nazi ideals and portray much of those views in their music and imagery. Thankfully, these musicians are largely shunned by prominent artists of the black metal community.
