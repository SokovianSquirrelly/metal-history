The Brutality of Death Metal

Death metal is an extreme subgenre of heavy metal, typically with heavily distorted and low-tuned guitars, aggressive drumming techniques such as double kicks and blast beats, palm muting and tremolo picking, aggressive, growling vocals, and often a minor, atonal, or chromatic chord progression.

Death metal started in response to thrash metal and early black metal in the early to mid-80s. Bands like Slayer and Venom were large influences on the death metal scene that started in America with bands like Possessed, Morbid Angel, Obituary, Autopsy, Cannibal Corpse, and Death. The genre peaked in the early-90s. However due to the media attention given to the controversial Norwegian black metal scene, death metal never really broke into the mainstream especially in America.

Music Example: Death - Born Dead

The subgenre did however spread to Sweden quickly where it developed its own death metal scene with bands like Grave, Carnage, God Macabre, and Entombed. These bands became gateway for melodic death metal, which combined the brutality that was American death metal with melodic or harmonized guitar riffs and sometimes higher-pitched harsh vocals. This opened up what was known as the "Gothenburg Metal Scene" since many of these melodic death, or "melodeath" bands originated such as In Flames, At the Gates, and Dark Tranquility. The melodeath scene spread across Europe and even into America in the late-90s and early-2000s with Swedish bands like Arch Enemy (my personal favorite) and Amon Amarth, Finnish bands like Children of Bodom, and American bands like The Black Dahlia Murder. This scene also went to inspire the rise of melodic metalcore in the 2000s, bringing metalcore bands like Killswitch Engage and As I Lay Dying into the mainstream.
